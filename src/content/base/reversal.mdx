## Reversal

A word or phrase becomes another when reversed. For example: <Example><Step><label>ONE</label> <span class="reverse">desserts</span></Step> <Finally><label>TWO</label> stressed</Finally></Example>.

<blockquote>
REVERSAL (2 4, 6) (TWO = NI3+ usage)

&ldquo;But Ham, Daddy told us to place in the Ark<br/>
A unicorn, minotaur, griffon, and snark.&rdquo;<br/>
&ldquo;Those critters are ONE, and they cannot be TWO.<br/>
There&rsquo;s too many now, Shem-we&rsquo;re taking the
shrew,<br/>
Giraffe, monkey, elephant, lion, and lamb.&rdquo;<br/>
If you mourn for the loss of the dragon-blame Ham.

=Dumbo, A.
</blockquote>

The solution: <Example><Step><label>ONE</label> <span class="reverse">de trop</span></Step> <Finally><label>TWO</label> ported</Finally></Example>.

In the **bigram reversal**, two-letter chunks are reversed instead of single letters. There aren&rsquo;t many of these; one example is <Example><Step><label>ONE</label> <span class="reverse">se-ra-ph</span></Step> <Finally><label>TWO</label> ph-ra-se</Finally></Example>.

If one or both parts are not dictionary entries, the puzzle is not a reversal but a <a href="base/mynoreteh" class="todo">**mynoreteh**</a>.
