## Curtailment

A word or phrase becomes another when its last letter is removed. Example: <Example><Step><label>ONE</label> sting~y~</Step> <Finally><label>TWO</label> sting</Finally></Example>.

> CURTAILMENT (8)
> 
> I&rsquo;m a young LONGER chemist, but so far I&rsquo;ve failed<br/>
> To discover a drug that&rsquo;s as good as CURTAILED.<br/>
> My motives are pure: to make the world healthy<br/>
> And in the process become really wealthy!
> 
> =Mr. Tex

The solution: <Example><Step><label>LONGER</label> aspirin~g~</Step> <Finally><label>FINAL</label> aspirin</Finally></Example>.

A curtailment may consist of more than two words, though these are rare. An obscure but dazzling example: <Example><Step definition="one who ascribes the Iliad and the Odyssey to different authors-look it up in NI2 if you don&rsquo;t believe it!"><label>ONE</label> chorizon~t~</Step> <Step definition="a particular layer of soil"><label>TWO</label> C-horizo~n~</Step> <Finally definition="a kind of spicy sausage"><label>THREE</label> chorizo</Finally></Example>.

In a **bigram curtailment**, a word or phrase becomes another when its last two letters are removed. For example, <Example><Step><label>ONE</label> satie~ty~</Step> <Finally><label>TWO</label> Satie</Finally></Example>.

In a **phonetic curtailment**, a word or phrase becomes another when its last sound is removed. For example: <Example><Step definition="as in &ldquo;on the qt&rdquo;"><label>ONE</label> qt</Step> <Step><label>TWO</label> cute</Step> <Finally><label>THREE</label> queue</Finally></Example>. For discussion of what constitutes a single sound, see <a href="modifier/phonetic">phonetic flats</a>.

In a **reversed curtailment**, after curtailing the first word, you reverse it to get the second. Example: <Example><Step><label>ONE</label> stink~y~</Step> <Finally><label>TWO</label> knits</Finally></Example>.
